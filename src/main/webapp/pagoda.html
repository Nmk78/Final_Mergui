Pagodas

Paw Daw Mu Pagoda

One of the most significant pagodas in Myeik is the Paw Daw Mu Pagoda. This ancient structure, believed to have been built in the 6th century BC, contains relics of the Buddha. It has undergone several restorations, the most notable being in 1931 by King Alaungpaya1. The pagoda is a symbol of peace and spirituality, and visitors often participate in the tradition of “aung myay nin chin,” making wishes while lightly stamping on its grounds.

Thein Daw Gyi

Thein Daw Gyi Pagoda is another important religious site in Myeik, offering a peaceful atmosphere and a stunning view of the surroundings. It is a perfect spot for meditation and quiet reflection.
